Find the Masked Man

CTF · ctf.osint.industries

Solved: [DATE HERE], 2026

Challenge Description

A photograph was taken in Paris on December 3rd, 2023, around 18:00. The mission was to identify the nearest metro station to the location where the masked man was photographed — using only contextual and visual clues. The scene was described as a central and upscale Parisian district, near the intersection of a Rue and an Avenue.

Flag format: OSINT{STATION_NAME} — all uppercase, no accents, underscores between words.

Initial Recon

The challenge gave me four key constraints to work with:

  • Date: December 3rd, 2023 — useful for matching seasonal lighting and any holiday decorations visible in the photo
  • Time: ~18:00 — early evening in Paris in December means it would already be fully dark, with streetlights and shop lights on
  • Area: central, upscale district of Paris — this narrows things to arrondissements like the 1st, 8th, 16th
  • Layout: intersection of a Rue and an Avenue — Avenues in Paris are typically wide, tree-lined, and often radiate from major landmarks

The mention of an Avenue was the biggest clue. Paris has a limited set of grand avenues in upscale districts — Avenue des Champs-Élysées, Avenue Montaigne, Avenue George V, Avenue Marceau, and a handful of others around the 8th and 16th arrondissements.

Lessons Learned

  • For Paris geolocation specifically, the arrondissement-by-arrondissement architectural style is a fast filter — Haussmann-era buildings, awning colors, and signage all narrow the search significantly
  • Time-of-year matters — December lighting and holiday decorations would be visible in the photo and need to be matched against street-level imagery taken in the same season
  • Avenues in Paris are far less common than Rues, so leading with "find the avenue first" is a much faster strategy than searching every street
  • Google Street View "time travel" (back-dating imagery) helps when current Street View doesn't match — Paris updates frequently, so a 2023 photo may need 2022–2023 imagery to align
